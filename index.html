00 // the question

Can a system improve itself — and prove it?_

Recursive self-improvement is now a named, mainstream research object: one of Google DeepMind's four pathways from AGI to superintelligence.

C1NAMED · UNSOLVED · 2026
DeepMind defines RSI as "AI facilitating AI research & development, thereby leading to improved AI systems, that, in turn, can facilitate research progress even more" — and concedes its dynamics are poorly understood: no historic precedent, no scaling law.
Genewein et al. (Google DeepMind), arXiv:2606.12683 — ref [14]

Meanwhile the only self-improving systems that actually run grade themselves on benchmarks. A benchmark score can be gamed by the very system it grades. A machine-checked proof cannot. This page asks one question: what does self-improvement mean when the referee is a proof checker? It turns out five separate research traditions spent ninety years answering it — and they barely cite each other.

01 // five traditions, ninety years

What is actually known_

I. Proof theory, 1931–1962 — the wall and the ladder

C2BLOCKED · LÖB 1955
No consistent theory trusts itself: the schema ∀φ: T ⊢ □T⌜φ⌝ → φ is impossible. Total self-certification is not difficult — it is inconsistent. (Gödel's second incompleteness theorem is the consistency special case.)
Löb, J. Symbolic Logic 20 (1955); Gödel, Monatshefte 38 (1931) — refs [01][02]
C3PROVED · 1939 / 1962
But what T cannot do to itself, its successor does to it: T + RFN(T) is strictly stronger, the step iterates along ordinals, and along suitable paths the progression is complete for arithmetic truth. Sound self-improvement of theories: proved, sixty years before the acronym RSI.
Turing, Proc. LMS (1939); Feferman, JSL 27 (1962) — refs [03][05]
C4PROVED · 1962
The catch, proved the same year: along other paths the progression stays incomplete forever, and completeness paths are non-effective — as hard to find as the truths being sought. Improvement is cheap; steering is the hard part.
Feferman & Spector, JSL 27 (1962) — ref [06]

II. Computability, 1938–1971 — license and speed limit

C5PROVED · KLEENE
Programs that operate on their own source code exist by fixed point — the recursion theorem licenses RSI's self-reference unconditionally. (What general self-inspection can conclude is a different matter: Rice.)
Kleene, Introduction to Metamathematics (1952) — ref [04]
C6PROVED · BLUM 1967/71
Some computable problems have no fastest program: an endless ladder of strictly faster programs provably exists — and no effective procedure climbs it. Endless improvement is real; computing your way along it is not. The exact mirror, in the speed dimension, of Feferman–Spector in the strength dimension.
Blum, JACM 14 (1967); Blum, JACM 18 (1971) — refs [07][08]

III. Agent foundations, 2003–2013 — the price list of self-trust

C7CONDITIONAL · 2003
Schmidhuber's Gödel machine rewrites its own code only upon a formal proof that the rewrite raises expected utility. Within its axioms, provably optimal — if such proofs can be found. In practice the gate almost never opens: the burden "prove it helps" is too heavy.
Schmidhuber, arXiv:cs/0309048 (2003) — ref [09]
C8PARTIAL · TILING 2013
Yudkowsky–Herreshoff pose self-modification as an agent constructing a successor it trusts — and hit the Löbian obstacle by name: an agent reasoning in T cannot in general trust a successor reasoning in T, so naive trust chains are finite ("running out of trust"). The patches are priced exactly: descending trust T–n is unsound on the standard numbers (they prove T–0 ⊢ ∃n:¬ψ(n)); Fallenstein's parametric polymorphism Tκ is sound but buys only "safe for κ steps". Bounded successor-trust is the going rate.
Yudkowsky & Herreshoff, MIRI (2013) — ref [10]

IV. Verified systems, 2011–2021 — the tower that runs

C9ENGINEERED · 2015
Milawa: a self-verifying theorem prover — a tower of increasingly powerful proof checkers, each layer verifying the next, with soundness proved down to the machine code that runs it. The reflective tower is not philosophy; it compiles. But it is static: assembled by hand, once — it discovers nothing.
Davis & Myreen, J. Automated Reasoning (2015); ITP (2011); cf. verified bootstrapped compilation, Myreen, CPP (2021) — refs [11][12]

V. Empirical ML, 2024–2026 — improvement without verification

C10REAL · UNVERIFIED · 2025
The Darwin Gödel Machine drops Schmidhuber's proof requirement for benchmark scores — and works: agents rewriting their own code went 20.0% → 50.0% on SWE-bench. The cost is stated in the paper itself: validation is empirical only, and self-modified code tends toward the uninterpretable. Improvement became real the moment it stopped being verifiable.
Zhang et al., arXiv:2505.22954 (2025) — ref [15]
C11DIAGNOSIS · 2026
And the field's own diagnosis points back at proof theory: sustained RSI needs genuine self-reference (Kleene fixed points), current LLMs sit below that "introspection threshold" (quasi-introspection, no reflexive access to themselves) — and the ceiling on self-trust is named outright: the Löbian obstacle.
arXiv:2607.04277 (2026) — ref [16]
Ninety years, five traditions, one shape: self-reference is licensed (Kleene), endless improvement exists (Blum, Feferman), full self-certification is forbidden (Löb), successor-trust is bounded (tiling), steering is non-effective (Feferman–Spector), bounded towers compile (Milawa) — and the systems that actually run today skip verification entirely (DGM). Assembled, this is the mathematics of RSI. It has simply never been run as one loop.

03 // the loop

Prove soundness. Measure benefit._

The design principle that splits Schmidhuber's impossible burden in two: every self-modification must carry a machine-checked certificate that it is sound — it cannot corrupt the verifier or admit a false theorem — while how much it helps is measured openly, in numbers anyone can rerun.

The discovered mathematics is not the product. It is the fuel. Three feedback ports:

04 // the missing theorem

Stated, not claimed_

Does there exist a computable operator I on verified provers such that, for every prover P in some infinite class:

(1) I(P) applies only self-proposed, self-certified modifications
Capped by Löb: full self-certification is inconsistent (C2), and successor-trust is bounded — descending chains run out, parametric polymorphism buys "safe for κ steps" only (C8). Any honest version of (1) must state its trust budget explicitly. Löb 1955; Yudkowsky–Herreshoff 2013
(2) strength(I(P)) > strength(P), measured by proof-theoretic ordinal
Achievable externally: reflective extension strictly raises strength (C3), and the measure is exact — reflection rank equals proof-theoretic ordinal; iterated reflection climbs the εα ladder. "It improved" can be a falsifiable sentence with an ordinal coordinate. Feferman 1962; Pakhomov & Walsh, JSL (2021)
(3) the run continues autonomously, forever
Blocked in general: completeness paths through the ordinal notations are non-effective (C4), and in the speed dimension the endless ladder provably cannot be climbed effectively (C6). Autonomy is exactly where the known mathematics says the hardness concentrates. Feferman–Spector 1962; Blum 1967/1971
The conjunction is open. Every known relaxation is charted above: drop (1) and you get Feferman's progressions; drop (2)'s verification and you get the Darwin Gödel Machine; drop (3) and you get Milawa's finite tower. Our artifact targets the honest finite instance: certified strength monotone along the run, as a machine-checked invariant — every step a theorem, the trust budget printed on the label.
05 // system limits

What this is not_

// PERMANENT PANEL — NOT A FOOTNOTE

  • Not a theory that proves itself. Inconsistent — Gödel (1931), Löb (1955). No architecture routes around a theorem.
  • Not unbounded autonomous ascent. Path choice is non-effective (Feferman–Spector 1962); effective speedup is impossible in general (Blum 1971). Our loop's trust budget is finite and stated.
  • Not a claim that today's LLMs self-improve. The diagnosis says they sit below the introspection threshold (arXiv:2607.04277).
  • Not machine-discovered new mathematics. v1 targets elementary arithmetic; the cited mathematics is 1931–2026 and belongs to its authors.
  • Not running yet. Verified counters read 0, in amber, above the fold of their own section — and stay that way until lake build says otherwise.
