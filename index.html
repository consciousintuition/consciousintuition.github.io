01 · The loop

Why feeding theorems back is self-improvement, not a slogan.

The claim has a causal chain, each link measurable. More verified nodes give the retriever more true premises; better premises raise the prover's close-rate; a higher close-rate yields more verified nodes. That is the loop gain — and it is falsifiable:

STAGE A
Verified DAG grows
every kernel-accepted theorem becomes a retrievable premise — growth is monotone by construction
STAGE B
Retrieval sharpens
premise selection draws on a strictly larger set of true, machine-checked lemmas
STAGE C
Close-rate rises
more proofs terminate within budget; certified procedures compress whole statement classes
STAGE D
Loop closes
new theorems re-enter as premises and tactics — the search space of round t+1 is shaped by round t

The metric that cannot be gamed: reach — the number of conjectures per round that fail under the previous library and succeed under the current one, where "succeed" means the kernel compiled the proof. Reach is computed from build logs alone.

FALSIFIER — if reach stays 0 across rounds, the loop is dead and this page will report exactly that.

TIER 1 — SUPERPOSITION GENERATORSTATUS: DESIGN

Neural Conjecture Generator

Spec. An LLM proposal head (to be fine-tuned on the Mathlib4 corpus) under MCTS over statement space, emitting candidate statements in Lean 4 syntax; a fast numeric counterexample search kills refutable candidates before any prover time is spent.

Function in the loop. Populates the state space with open conjectures — boxes with unmeasured cats. Temperature controls distance from the training distribution.

TIER 2 — OBSERVATION OPERATORSTATUS: DESIGN

Hybrid Proof Searcher

Spec. Neural premise selection over the verified DAG feeding a portfolio of symbolic backends — duper, aesop, omega, norm_num, decide, tactic scripts — racing to close each proof term.

Function in the loop. Attempted measurement. Only lake build collapses |ψ⟩: kernel acceptance or nothing. A benchmark can be gamed by its own contestant; the kernel cannot.

TIER 3 — EPISTEMIC ASCENTSTATUS: DESIGN

Reflective Self-Improvement

Spec. Each new theorem is compiled, vectorized, and injected into the retrieval store and premise-selection model; discovered decision procedures are installed as tactics only with a kernel-checked soundness certificate (proof by reflection).

Function in the loop. The system grows its own vocabulary; each round reshapes the next round's search. The changelog is a sequence of theorems.

03 · Design constraints

Gödel and Löb are the specification.

Two invariants hold at every iteration, by construction — they are why the loop's claims can be strict where everyone else's are statistical.

Löb invariance — the fixed referee

Blanket self-trust is inconsistent, not merely hard: if T ⊢ □⌜φ⌝→φ then T ⊢ φ (Löb 1955; Gödel II is the φ=⊥ corollary). So no level here ever certifies itself. Discovered proofs and installed procedures live at L0, certified by theorems of L1 — Lean's logic, fixed and external. Trust in L1 is an explicitly stated assumption, one honest level above what any system can prove about itself.

L0 · discovered proofs & installed tactics certified at L1
L1 · Lean 4 kernel fixed · external · immutable
L2 · kernel soundness assumed — stated, never claimed proved

Self-modification never touches the kernel. The one fatal failure — installing a change that admits a false theorem — is structurally unreachable: every change enters through the gate or not at all.

Strict constructivism — total transparency

Every accepted proof ships its exact axiom footprint; the audit gate rejects Classical.choice, any sorry, any unjustified axiom.

-- audit gate, run on every accepted theorem
#print axioms SC.discovered_theorem_00000
⊢ [propext, Quot.sound]   -- constructive ✓ admitted

Failures are first-class: counterexamples, kernel errors and audit rejections are published with the same fidelity as theorems. And one scope line, stated rather than hidden: v1 improves capability at fixed logical strength — the ambient theory never changes, so no ordinal moves; any future reflection step that would change that will arrive as a machine-checked artifact or not at all.
