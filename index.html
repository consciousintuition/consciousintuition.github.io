01 · The problem

Self-improvement exists. A theory of it does not.

Three failures define the frontier — all three are failures of missing mathematics, not missing compute.

P1 — GENERALIZATION

Learning does not transfer

Systems that master a distribution collapse outside it — and the field measures generalization with the same benchmarks it trains against. A foundation that made "concept" and "transfer" into mathematical objects would turn an empirical mystery into a provable property.

Missing: a transfer map t : Struct(A) → Struct(B) with a proved invariant — today "transfer" has no definition to even fail.

P2 — UNVERIFIED RSI

The loop grades itself

The self-improving systems that run today validate their own rewrites empirically — the judge is a score the contestant can optimize against. Improvement without an ungameable referee is a claim, not a fact. A proof kernel is the one referee that cannot be gamed.

And self-certification is closed by theorem: if T ⊢ □φ → φ then T ⊢ φ (Löb 1955) — blanket self-trust collapses.

P3 — THE GAP ITSELF

RSI → ASI has no theory

The best surveys of the road to superintelligence name recursive improvement as a pathway and admit its dynamics are poorly understood — no precedent, no scaling law. Whether the loop compounds, saturates or oscillates is exactly the kind of question only mathematics answers.

Intelligence has an exact definition — Υ(π) = Σμ 2−K(μ)Vπμ (Legg–Hutter 2007) — but it is incomputable; benchmarks are its gameable shadow.

02 · The direction

Evolve the theory, not the weights.

One idea drives this program — stated as a research direction, not a product claim.

The unit of evolution is a mathematical theory — its definitions and axioms — not a parameter vector. New structures are proposed in the landscape where foundations are actively rebuilt (univalent foundations, higher structures); bridges between theories — the maps that carry proofs from one world into another — are both the evolutionary operator and the measure of depth: a theory that connects widely is a theory that matters.

TIER A — NOW. The loop proves kernel-checked theorems about a formal model of itself — monotone library growth, terminating filters, sound tactic procedures — and a self-modification is admitted only on the strength of such a theorem. Self-knowledge as the admission ticket. No Löbian obstruction: these are theorems about a model of the loop, not the referee certifying itself.

TIER B — THE PRIZE. The conditional scaling-law theorem: under which conditions does reach(e) = #{ goals provable under Le and not under Le−1 } stay positive? What PAC theory did for "learning," done for verified discovery — and discovered, if at all, by the loop itself, operating on its own telemetry turned into formal objects.

TIER C — THE BOUNDARY. What this theory can never contain: the soundness of its own referee (Löb 1955; Gödel 1931). Stated, never dodged — the ascent lives one level up, always.

03 · Research agenda — the edge

Five open problems. One falsifiable hypothesis.

This is a research program, not a product. Each question below sits at the current edge of the field, each carries a named obstruction — and the central hypothesis can fail in public.

CENTRAL RESEARCH QUESTION · RQ0

Can an RSI system computationally discover the mathematical theory of its own improvement — and which fragment of that theory is reachable at each tier? (A: self-model theorems, now · B: the conditional scaling law, the prize · C: its referee's soundness — never, by Löb.)

SYMBOLIC VISUAL — PROOF SEARCH: BRANCHES EXPLORED · DEAD ENDS RETRACTED (BACKTRACKING) · ONE PATH KERNEL-LOCKED
CENTRAL HYPOTHESIS · H1

reach(e) = #{ goals provable under Le and not under Le−1 } stays positive epoch after epoch — measured from kernel logs only.

FALSIFIER — if reach flatlines at 0, the loop is search, not self-improvement, and this page will say so.

PROPOSED INSTRUMENT (not yet in force) — reach counted mechanically, no self-judgment: a result scores only if library search (exact?) cannot derive it in one step, the tactic battery cannot close it instantly, and the prover closes it within a fixed budget. Applied retroactively, this is expected to demote most of epochs 1–4 — and the dashboard will show the demotions.

RQ1 · VALUE

What makes a theorem worth proving?

Triviality is cheap — x + 0 = x compiles perfectly — yet mathematical depth has no accepted definition. Our proxy, reach, scores what a result unlocks, not what it says. Nearest formal handle: proof-theoretic ordinal analysis (Pakhomov–Walsh, JSL 2021). Open.

→ RQ0 needs this: without a value function, the loop cannot know which theorems about itself matter.

RQ2 · TRANSFER

Can generalization become a theorem?

A provable transfer map t : Struct(A) → Struct(B) whose invariant survives the crossing — today "transfer" in ML has no definition precise enough to fail. Functorial semantics exists; machine-discovered transfer maps do not. Open.

→ RQ0 needs this: a theory of improvement is a theory of what transfers between epochs.

RQ3 · TRUST

How much self-trust is affordable?

Löb caps blanket self-trust (T ⊢ □φ→φ forces T ⊢ φ); tiling agents pay for each successor in strength. The exact trust budget of a loop that never certifies its own referee is uncharted (Yudkowsky–Herreshoff 2013). Open.

→ RQ0's Tier-C boundary IS this budget — how close to self-reference the theory may go.

RQ4 · LOOP GAIN

Does a bigger library search better?

Monotone growth is trivial; better search is not. Under what conditions does verified memory raise the next epoch's close-rate — and when does it saturate? AIDE² showed the code-level analogue empirically (2026); nothing verified, nothing mathematical. Open.

→ RQ0's Tier-B theorem IS the loop-gain law — this question stated formally.

RQ5 · FORMALIZATION

Does intuition survive translation?

Our agents emit reasoning and Lean (D3). When the kernel rejects, which layer failed — the intuition or its translation? Faithful autoformalization at research level is unsolved; the Formal Conjectures benchmark (Firsching et al. 2026) measures exactly this gap — and this program no longer just cites it: we run a harness against its frozen evaluation sets, pinned to the exact snapshot its published baselines used (§03·B below). Open.

→ RQ0's evidence chain breaks here if the loop's self-description is not faithfully formalized.

Known obstructions — and the countermeasures we commit to

C1 · Well-typed terms are sparseNo free generation: pre-typed statement families, kernel-error repair loop, cheap type-only precheck — quarantined, never counted as knowledge.
C2 · Triviality trapFitness = reach. Anything the old library already proves scores zero; sub-100ms simp/decide closures score zero. Value is measured by what a result unlocks.
C3 · Kernel latencyBatched candidates per lake call, a persistent Lean server, and a three-stage funnel: numeric filter (µs) → type check → full proof only for survivors.
C4 · Axiom escapeStructurally closed: axiom and sorryAx rejected at the gate; footprint whitelist {propext, Quot.sound}; proposed axioms live in quarantine — explorable, never knowledge.
C5 · No intermediate conceptsDefinitions are first-class records; a definition's fitness is the reach of the theorems that use it. Vocabulary no verified theorem consumes never enters the library.

When: "within an episode, at test time, or after deployment" — ours: discrete epochs. How: "reward or value learning, imitation, evolutionary search" — ours: propose / critique / prove over shared verified memory. Where: "web and UI, games, robotics, science, enterprise" — ours: formal mathematics. Alignment & security: "long horizon stability, regression risk" — ours: an immutable kernel gate and axiom quarantine. Evaluation: the workshop names the missing piece — "methods that make self-improvement measurable" — ours: kernel verdicts, never benchmarks.
